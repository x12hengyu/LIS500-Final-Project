Event Rules/Policies


This workshop is dedicated to providing a welcoming and supportive environment for all people, regardless of background or identity. By participating in this workshop, participants accept to abide by this Code of Conduct and accept the procedures by which any Code of Conduct incidents are resolved. We do not tolerate behavior that is disrespectful or that excludes, intimidates, or causes discomfort to others. We do not tolerate discrimination or harassment based on characteristics that include, but are not limited to , gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, citizenship, nationality, ethnic or social origin, pregnancy, familial status, veteran status, genetic information, religion or belief (or lack thereof), membership of a national minority, property, age, education, socio-economic status, technical choices, and experience level.

Expected Behavior

  • Communicate openly and share issues with the team
    1. Members of the group should be open to communication. Members are expected to discuss any ideas, issues, and questions that are related to our group project to everyone in this group. Private communication is discouraged during the meeting. We encourage a more united group.
  • Use consensus for team decisions
    1. When we encounter a problem we should collaborate and reach a consensus. The group's consensus should be the final choice, not an individual idea.
  • Actively participate in meetings
    1. Group members are expected to speak and share ideas actively during group meetings.
  • Use welcoming and inclusive language
    1. Show courtesy and respect towards other members. Members should use a more inclusive, neutral language when communicating. Everyone should avoid offensive language at meetings or when not in meetings.
  • Be respectful of different viewpoints and experiences
    1. Members are expected to respect the preferences and ideas of others. When there is a different point of view, listen carefully to understand the other person's ideas. Do not use excessive language to offend the other person. Share your own understanding after you understand each other's ideas and discuss the consensus together.
  • Gracefully accept constructive criticism
    1. Learn to accept criticism from others. The combination of your own idea and criticism from others will help to build a more constructive result.

Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants at any point during the event include:

  • Unwelcome remarks, gestures or physical contact
  • Insults or put downs
  • Sexist, racist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or exclusionary jokes
  • Violent threats or language directed against another person
  • Continuing to initiate interaction (including photography or recording) with someone after being asked to stop
  • The display or circulation of offensive, derogatory or sexually explicit pictures or other materials, including by email and on the Internet
  • Written or verbal comments which have the effect of excluding people

Consequences of Unacceptable Behavior

If you see someone violating the Code of Conduct, we ask that you report it to any of the workshop organizers. We value the involvement of everyone and are committed to creating a friendly and respectful environment for learning, teaching, and contributing. All participants are expected to show respect and courtesy to others.

Everyone participating in this conference and its activities is required to conform to the Code of Conduct. This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces affiliated with the conference. Workshop organizers are expected to assist with the enforcement of the Code of Conduct.

Participants who are asked to stop any inappropriate behavior are expected to comply immediately. This applies to any workshop events. If a participant engages in behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, the organizers may warn the offender, ask them to leave the event, or investigate the Code of Conduct violation and impose appropriate sanctions.
